Teams on the Web — Browser Compliance Enforcement
HIGH Both
📅Deadline: May 15, 2026
🔖MC1230888
Starting May 15, 2026, Microsoft Teams on the web will only support browsers compliant with ECMAScript 2022. Users on non-compliant browsers will be blocked entirely — not degraded — after the enforcement date. Teams desktop and mobile clients are not affected.
Tech River Action
Audit browser versions via NinjaRMM across all managed clients. Identify any users hitting Teams via web (especially BYOD). Push browser update policies now. Direct heavily browser-dependent users to the Teams desktop client as a resilient alternative. Send proactive user communication before May 1.
Legacy Teams Phone Device Retirement
HIGH Both
📅Deadline: June 1, 2026
🔖MC1229952
Certain legacy Teams Phone devices (specific Poly Trio, Yealink, and Crestron models) will be retired June 1, 2026. Affected devices cannot sign in or make/receive calls after that date. There is no software fix — physical hardware replacement is the only path forward.
Tech River Action
Cross-reference MC1229952's device list against all client hardware inventories in Ninja/Hudu immediately. Any match requires active procurement planning now — hardware lead times can consume your runway. Loop in account managers to set client budget expectations.
Microsoft 365 Pricing Increase — All Commercial SKUs
HIGH MSP/Admin
📅Effective: July 1, 2026
🔖Partner Center / Microsoft Blog
All major M365 commercial SKUs are increasing July 1, 2026. F1: $2.25→$3, F3: $8→$10, E3: +$3/user/month, E5: $57→$60. Business Basic, Standard, and Premium are also affected. Customers renewing before July 1 can lock in current pricing until their next renewal.
Tech River Action
Pull all client renewal dates against the July 1 cutoff. Start proactive renewal conversations now, especially for Business Premium and E3 clients. Frame around value-adds (Defender for Office P1, Intune capabilities now bundled). This is an active upsell opportunity, not just a cost warning.
SharePoint OTP Authentication Retirement → Entra B2B
MEDIUM-HIGH Both
📅Transition: May 2026 | Retirement: Jul–Aug 2026
🔖MC1243549
Microsoft is retiring SharePoint One-Time Passcode (OTP) authentication for external file sharing. Beginning May 2026, new invitations will use Entra B2B. Starting July 2026, external users without a B2B guest account will be denied access on previously shared links.
Tech River Action
For any client sharing SharePoint/OneDrive externally (vendors, accountants, clients), review external sharing reports in the SharePoint Admin Center. Identify OTP-authenticated external users. Ensure Entra B2B email OTP is not disabled. Proactively communicate the change — this will generate helpdesk tickets if ignored.
